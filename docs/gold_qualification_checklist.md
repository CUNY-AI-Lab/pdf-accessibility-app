# Gold Qualification Checklist

Updated: 2026-04-06

This checklist is stricter than:

- "passes `veraPDF`"
- "has `/Title` and `/Lang`"
- "looks tagged"

It is the bar for promoting an externally sourced PDF into the round-trip gold corpus.

## Status labels

- `rejected`
  - fails a hard gate or clearly lacks trustworthy accessibility quality
- `screened`
  - passed provenance and validator checks, but practical accessibility review is still too thin
- `provisional_gold_candidate`
  - passed hard gates and a meaningful human spot-check, but still needs one more targeted content check before corpus promotion
- `qualified_gold`
  - strong enough to seed or extend the round-trip corpus

## Stage 1: Hard gates

All of these must pass.

- independent provenance
  - not generated by this app
  - not a local remediation probe
  - source URL or source institution is known
- valid PDF payload
  - actual PDF file, not an HTML error page or wrapper
- explicit validator pass
  - `veraPDF --flavour ua1` passes cleanly
- document fit
  - matches a target corpus lane such as report, article, manual, or table-heavy technical document

Failure on any Stage 1 item means `rejected`.

## Stage 2: Practical accessibility spot-check

This is the minimum human review.

### A. Front matter

- title page is visually coherent
- extracted title matches the visible title closely enough to be useful
- document language is set and plausible
- no obvious glyph corruption on the cover or first text page

### B. Navigation

- at least one navigation signal is present and sane
  - bookmarks, or
  - visible table of contents with usable destinations, or
  - dense internal link structure that clearly supports navigation
- heading structure appears plausible on at least one early page

### C. Reading order

- one early body page reads in a sane order
- one later body page also reads in a sane order
- no obvious artifacts dominate the assistive reading experience

### D. Content-type check

Choose the most relevant one for the document.

- table-heavy document
  - inspect at least one table page for sane header/body presentation
- figure-heavy document
  - inspect at least one figure page for caption/figure relationship
- form document
  - inspect at least one page for navigable fields and labels
- ordinary prose/report
  - inspect at least one dense body page with lists, notes, or sectional structure

### E. Accessibility quality sanity

- no obvious placeholder or junk metadata
- no evidence that accessibility is only nominal or mechanically tagged
- no glaring mismatch between visible structure and extracted reading structure

Passing Stage 1 plus a thin Stage 2 review yields `screened`.
Passing Stage 1 plus all of Stage 2 yields `provisional_gold_candidate`.

## Stage 3: Promotion readiness

This is the final check before corpus promotion.

- candidate adds useful coverage beyond existing golds
  - not just another near-duplicate unless duplication is intentional
- expected benchmark lane is clear
  - prose/manual
  - research/article/report
  - table-heavy technical
  - form
- candidate-specific recoverable assertions are easy to write
- no unresolved doubt about provenance or practical accessibility quality

Passing Stage 3 yields `qualified_gold`.

## Required evidence to record

Every external candidate should have a short note capturing:

- source URL
- provenance summary
- `veraPDF` result
- page count
- visible title and `/Title`
- `/Lang`
- whether bookmarks are present
- rough link/navigation density
- pages manually spot-checked
- final status label

## Promotion guidance

For this project, prefer new golds in this order:

1. long-form academic / research / technical reports
2. journal-like or conference-paper documents
3. table-heavy research/technical documents
4. forms

Forms are still useful, but they are not the primary baseline for the app.
